# Hawaiian Butter Mochi

A chewy, custardy coconut mochi cake made with mochiko (glutinous rice flour) — a Hawaiian potluck classic. Naturally gluten-free. Makes one 9×13 in pan, about 24 squares.

## Ingredients

- 454 g mochiko (glutinous / sweet rice flour), about 1 lb
- 400 g granulated sugar
- 10 g baking powder
- 400 g coconut milk (about one 13.5 oz can)
- 360 g evaporated milk (about one 12 oz can)
- 113 g unsalted butter, melted
- 200 g eggs (about 4 large)
- 8 g vanilla extract
- Optional: 30 g shredded coconut, for topping

**Total batter:** approximately 1,945 g

## Steps

### Step 1 — Prepare the Oven and Pan

#### Recipe

Preheat the oven to **175°C (350°F)**. Grease a **9×13 in (23×33 cm)** baking pan well.

### Step 2 — Whisk the Dry Ingredients

#### Recipe

In a large bowl, whisk together the mochiko, sugar, and baking powder.

### Step 3 — Whisk the Wet Ingredients

#### Recipe

In another bowl, whisk the eggs, then whisk in the coconut milk, evaporated milk, melted butter, and vanilla until smooth.

### Step 4 — Combine

#### Recipe

Pour the wet mixture into the dry and whisk until **completely smooth with no lumps**.

### Step 5 — Fill the Pan

#### Recipe

Pour the batter into the prepared pan. If using, sprinkle shredded coconut evenly over the top.

### Step 6 — Bake

#### Recipe

Bake at **175°C (350°F)** for about **1 hour**, until the top is golden and the center is set (no longer jiggly). A slower oven may need up to **1 hour 10 minutes**.

### Step 7 — Cool

#### Recipe

Cool completely in the pan — at least **1–2 hours** — before cutting into squares. Cutting it warm gives a gummy, sticky mess.

## Instructor Tips

- Cool it fully before cutting; warm butter mochi is gluey and won't hold clean edges.
- A plastic knife (or a knife wiped clean between cuts) gives the neatest squares.
- Mochiko — glutinous / sweet rice flour — is essential; regular rice flour or wheat flour won't give the chewy mochi texture.
- It's even chewier the next day. Store covered at room temperature.

## Troubleshooting

### Why is my butter mochi gummy and hard to cut?

It was almost certainly cut too soon. Let it cool completely — a few hours — so the starch sets and the squares hold their shape.

**Related topics:** cooling, cutting, texture

### Can I use regular rice flour instead of mochiko?

No. Only glutinous / sweet rice flour (mochiko) gives the stretchy, chewy crumb. Regular rice flour or wheat flour will not work.

**Related topics:** mochiko, ingredients, texture

### Why is the center still wet?

It's underbaked. Bake until the middle is set and no longer jiggles — up to about 1 hour 10 minutes in a slower oven.

**Related topics:** bake time, doneness

### How do I store it?

Cover at room temperature for 2–3 days; it firms up but stays chewy. Avoid the fridge, which makes it hard.

**Related topics:** storage, texture
