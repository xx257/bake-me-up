# Japanese Cheesecake

A light and airy Japanese-style cheesecake made with cream cheese and a medium-soft peak meringue. Baked in a water bath for a delicate, soufflé-like texture.

## Ingredients

### Batter
- 150 g cream cheese
- 50 g unsalted butter
- 83 g milk
- 30 g cake flour, sifted
- 30 g cornstarch
- 150 g eggs (about 5 extra-large eggs), separated
- 6 g vanilla extract (optional)
- 60 g sugar

## Steps

### Step 1 — Prepare Ingredients

#### Recipe

Leave all ingredients at room temperature. Separate the eggs into yolks and whites.

---

### Step 2 — Make the Cream Cheese Base

#### Recipe

Combine the cream cheese, butter, and milk in a saucepan. Warm gently over low heat while whisking until smooth and approximately 50°C (122°F). Remove from the heat.

Whisk in the sifted cake flour and cornstarch until smooth. Add the egg yolks and vanilla extract, then whisk until fully incorporated.

Strain the mixture into a clean mixing bowl to remove any lumps.

---

### Step 3 — Make the Meringue

#### Recipe

Whip the egg whites until foamy. Gradually add the sugar and continue whipping until the meringue reaches a medium-soft peak.

The meringue should hold its shape but still have a slight curl at the tip.

---

### Step 4 — Fold the Batter

#### Recipe

Whisk one-third of the meringue into the cream cheese mixture to lighten it.

Gently fold in the remaining meringue in two additions until no large streaks remain. Avoid overmixing to preserve volume.

---

### Step 5 — Pan and Prepare

#### Recipe

Pour the batter into a parchment-lined 6-inch cake pan.

Tap the pan gently on the counter and stir through the batter with a skewer to release large air bubbles.

Place the cake pan into a larger roasting pan.

Add hot water at approximately 80°C (175°F) until the water bath is about 1 inch deep. The water should be hot but not boiling.

---

### Step 6 — Bake

#### Recipe

Bake at 150°C (300°F) for 30 minutes.

Reduce the oven temperature to 140°C (285°F) and continue baking for another 30 minutes.

The cheesecake should be lightly colored, gently set, and still slightly jiggly in the center.

---

### Step 7 — Cool and Serve

#### Recipe

Remove the cake from the oven and unmold while still warm.

Peel away the parchment paper and allow the cheesecake to cool.

Serve warm, at room temperature, or chilled.

## Instructor Tips

- Watch the meringue carefully. Medium-soft peaks create the signature light texture.
- Fold gently. Overmixing will deflate the batter.
- Straining the batter helps produce a smoother crumb.
- Use hot but not boiling water in the water bath.
- Tap the pan and remove large air bubbles before baking for a cleaner interior.

## Troubleshooting

### Why did my cheesecake crack?

Cracks are usually caused by an oven that is too hot, an insufficient water bath, or overmixed batter. Ensure the water bath remains hot throughout baking and avoid overwhipping the meringue.

**Related topics:** oven temperature, water bath, meringue

### Why did my cheesecake collapse?

Some settling is normal. Significant collapse can happen when the cake is underbaked, the meringue is unstable, or the batter was overmixed during folding.

**Related topics:** meringue, baking time, folding

### Why are there large holes inside the cake?

Large holes are often caused by trapped air bubbles. Tap the pan before baking and run a skewer through the batter to release excess air.

**Related topics:** batter preparation, meringue

## Recipe Summary

- Yield: 1 six-inch cheesecake
- Style: Japanese soufflé cheesecake
- Bake: 150°C (300°F) for 30 minutes, then 140°C (285°F) for 30 minutes
- Water bath: approximately 80°C (175°F), 1 inch deep
- Serve warm, room temperature, or chilled
