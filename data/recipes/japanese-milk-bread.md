# Japanese Milk Bread (Shokupan)

- **Yield:** 1 loaf (12 slices)
- **Active time:** 40 min
- **Total time:** ~4 hr including proofing
- **Source:** Example recipe (uses the tangzhong method)

## Ingredients

### Tangzhong (water roux)
- 20g bread flour
- 60g water
- 40g whole milk

### Dough
- 250g bread flour
- 40g granulated sugar
- 5g salt
- 4g instant yeast
- 1 large egg (about 50g)
- 90g whole milk, lukewarm
- 30g unsalted butter, softened

### Finish
- 1 egg, beaten (egg wash)

## Steps

1. Make the tangzhong: whisk the 20g flour, water, and milk in a small saucepan over
   medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens into a smooth paste (~65°C /
   lines drawn with the whisk hold). Scrape into a bowl and cool to room temperature.
   (Timing: 5 min active, then cool ~20 min)
2. Combine the dough: mix bread flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Add the egg, lukewarm
   milk, and the cooled tangzhong. Knead until it comes together into a shaggy dough.
   (Timing: 10 min active)
3. Add butter a little at a time, kneading until the dough is smooth, elastic, and
   passes the windowpane test (stretch a piece thin enough to see light through it
   without tearing).
   (Timing: 10 min active)
4. First proof: cover and let rise until doubled.
   (Timing: hands-off ~60 min at warm room temp)
5. Shape: divide into 3 equal pieces, roll each flat, fold, and roll into cylinders.
   Place seam-down in a greased loaf pan.
   (Timing: 10 min active)
6. Second proof: cover and let rise until the dough crowns just below the pan rim.
   (Timing: hands-off ~50 min)
7. Bake: brush with egg wash and bake at 180°C (350°F) until deep golden and the loaf
   sounds hollow when tapped.
   (Timing: 30 min bake)
8. Cool in the pan 5 min, then turn out onto a rack and cool completely before slicing.
   (Timing: hands-off ~60 min)

## Notes

- The tangzhong must be fully cooled before it goes into the dough, or it kills the
  yeast and melts the butter unevenly.
- This is a wet, tacky dough — resist adding extra flour. It firms up once the butter
  is fully worked in. Sticky-but-smooth is correct; dry is over-floured.
- Windowpane is the real doneness cue for kneading; time is only a guide.
- "Crowns just below the rim" on the second proof is key — over-proofed dough collapses
  in the oven, under-proofed stays dense.
- Cold kitchen? Proof in an oven with just the light on. Proofing can take noticeably
  longer than the times above.
- Keeps soft 2–3 days at room temp in an airtight bag; freezes well sliced.
